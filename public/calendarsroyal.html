Details of the Three Calendars

His Majesty's Government recognises three calendars for official use in the kingdom, as follows. All dates are reckoned with respect to Coordinated Universal Time (UTC), and assume a position on the earth's surface of 0°N 0°E.

The Cyprian Calendar

The Cyprian Calendar is the primary calendar of the kingdom. It is used mostly when dating formal and legal documents.

The Cyprian Calendar is essentially the Hebrew or Jewish calendar, with the following modifications:

  • The usual twelve months are renamed, from the earliest (formerly Nisan) to the latest (formerly Adar): Primilis, Sectilis, Tertilis, Qartilis, Quintilis, Sextilis, September, October, November, December, Unodecember, Duodecember.
  • Instead of the system in the Hebrew calendar where, when an intercalary month needs to be added to a year, the month of Adar is replaced with the months I Adar and II Adar, the new month of Intercalaris is inserted between Duodecember and the following year's Primilis.

The Gregorian Calendar

The Gregorian Calendar is a secondary calendar of the kingdom, but it is used more frequently than the other two, due to its widespread use throughout the world.

The Sacred Calendar

The Sacred Calendar is another secondary calendar of the kingdom. It is used for calculating seasons and feasts relating to the Christian religion.

The Sacred Calendar is a simplified version of the liturgical calendar of the Latin Rite of the Roman Catholic Church. More specifically, with respect to important days:

  • Advent Sunday falls on the fourth Sunday before Christmas;
  • Christmas falls on the twenty-fifth day of December in the Gregorian Calendar;
  • Epiphany falls on the first Sunday following New Year's Day of the Gregorian Calendar;
  • Easter falls on the day on which it is determined to fall by the Bishop of Rome;
  • Ash Wednesday falls exactly forty-six days before Easter;
  • Palm Sunday falls on the Sunday before Easter;
  • Maundy Thursday falls on the Thursday before Easter;
  • Good Friday falls on the Friday before Easter;
  • Ascension falls on the sixth Thursday after Easter;
  • Pentecost falls on the seventh Sunday after Easter (not including Easter itelf);
  • All Saints' Day falls on the first day of November in the Gregorian Calendar;
  • All Souls' Day falls on the second day of November in the Gregorian Calendar.

With respect to seasons:

  • Advent lasts from dawn on Advent Sunday until sunset on the day before Christmas;
  • Christmas lasts from sunset on the day before Christmas until sunset on Epiphany;
  • The first period of Ordinary Time lasts from sunset on Epiphany until dawn on Ash Wednesday;
  • Lent lasts from dawn on Ash Wednesday until dawn on Easter;
  • Holy Week lasts from dawn on Palm Sunday until dawn on Easter;
  • The season of Easter lasts from dawn on Easter until sunset on Pentecost;
  • The second period of Ordinary Time lasts from sunset on Pentecost until dawn on Advent Sunday.

With respect to colours:

  • The colour of Ordinary Time is green;
  • The colour of Advent is violet;
  • The colour of Christmas is white;
  • The colour of Lent, except for Holy Week, is violet;
  • The colour of Holy Week is red;
  • The colour of Easter is white;
  • The colour of All Saints' Day is white;
  • The colour of All Souls' Day is violet.
